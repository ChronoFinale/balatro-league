## What Is Team Tour?

Team Tour is a team-based Balatro Multiplayer (BMP) tournament. Players sign up,
captains draft teams, and then those teams battle it out through a regular season
before heading into playoffs.

Every week you'll play a 1v1 **set** against someone around your seed. A set is a
series of games between two players, usually a best-of-3. **Tournament Organizers
(TOs)** help run the tournament, answer questions, and handle disputes when needed.

Win your set, help your team win the week, and earn some bragging rights.

This event is **not** affiliated with the Balatro Multiplayer server or staff.

The goal is simple: play competitive Balatro, improve, meet people, and have fun.

---

## Teams, Captains, and Draft

Before the season starts, captains draft players onto their teams. The draft uses a
**snake format**:

- Captains take turns selecting players.
- Draft order is determined by the TOs.
- Captains are part of their own team and occupy a seed based on their ranking.
- The draft continues until every team is full.

The draft may take a few days depending on schedules and time zones.

---

## Seeds

A player's **seed** is determined by their position in their team's draft order:

- First drafted player = Seed 1
- Second drafted player = Seed 2
- Third drafted player = Seed 3
- …and so on.

Seeds are used to help create fair weekly pairings. Players will generally only be
paired against opponents **within two seeds** of themselves:

- Seed 4 can play Seeds 2–6.
- Seed 1 can play Seeds 1–3.

---

## Season Format

The exact number of teams and players per team depends on signups.

- Teams are divided into **conferences**.
- The regular-season schedule is announced before the season begins.
- Each player is expected to play **one set per week**.
- A team wins the week by winning **at least 51%** of its sets:
  - 7-player teams need **4** set wins.
  - 9-player teams need **5** set wins.
  - 11-player teams need **6** set wins.

---

## Weekly Pairings

Each week, captains create the set pairings, alternating back and forth:

1. Captain A sends a player.
2. Captain B responds with a player.
3. Captain B sends the next player.
4. Captain A responds.
5. Repeat until all pairings are complete.

**Substitutions** are allowed if needed. If a player must be substituted mid-week,
captains may create a new set pairing for the substitute.

---

## Scheduling

Once pairings are posted, reach out to your opponent and find a time to play. Please
use **Hammertime** when sharing times — time zones are confusing enough already:
<https://hammertime.cyou/>

- Try to have your set **scheduled by Thursday at 11:59 PM EST/EDT.**
- If someone refuses to communicate or make a reasonable effort to schedule, they may
  receive a **0–2 set loss**.
- If your original time falls through, try to reschedule if you can. Nobody is required
  to accept a reschedule, but we encourage people to work together when they reasonably
  can.

Playing a set **after** its assigned week is allowed but should only happen as a last
resort. If it does, make sure your **opponent**, **both captains**, and a **TO** all
know. Sets can only be delayed by **one week** and should be completed as soon as
possible. TOs may assign a forfeit if a delayed set remains unresolved for an extended
period or is holding up the tournament.

## Set Rules

Sets are **best-of-3 (BO3)** by default — first to **2 game wins** takes the set.
Players may agree to play a **best-of-5 (BO5)**, but the result is still converted to
a BO3 result for Team Tour scoring.

**Standard settings**

- Standard lobby
- 4 lives
- PvP starts at **Ante 2**
- Ranked ruleset
- Ranked timer (**150**) — players may agree to a different timer

Approved mods are the same as those allowed in Ranked; cosmetic mods are generally
fine. Approved mod list:
<https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1GLhYCSzx4dhJfK8wPBjaZRsLArqhWW7ruemCvNr4228/edit?usp=sharing>

## Pick / Ban

Before each set, players determine which **deck/stake combination** they'll use
through a pick/ban phase. Each set draws from a pool of **9 deck/stake combinations**:

1. Flip a coin.
2. The winner chooses whether they want to **ban first**.
3. The first player **bans 1** combo.
4. The second player **bans 3** combos.
5. The first player **bans 3** combos.
6. The second player **chooses between the final 2** remaining combos.

The selected deck/stake combination is used for the set.

## Restarts, Disconnects, and Other Issues

- If **both players agree**, a game can be restarted for any reason.
- If someone disconnects **before** the first PvP blind, the game may be replayed.
- If someone disconnects **after** the first PvP blind, the game win goes to the player
  who stayed connected.
- If something strange happens that isn't covered by the rules, **ask a TO** — that's
  why they're here.

## Streaming and Coaching

Streaming your set is allowed. You **may** read chat **between** games. You may **not**
read chat **during** gameplay.

**Live coaching is not allowed.** This includes:

- Asking for advice during a game
- Receiving advice during a game
- Reading chat during gameplay
- Listening to commentary during gameplay

Other people are welcome to stream or commentate sets, but players may not use those
streams while playing. **Stream sniping is not allowed.** Don't do it.

---

## Reporting Scores

- Post your set result in the **results channel** after the set.
- **Both players** should react to the result to confirm it's correct.
- Players are responsible for checking that their scores are reported correctly.

---

## Playoffs

The **top 4 teams from each conference** qualify for playoffs. Playoffs last **3
weeks** and use a **single-elimination** bracket:

- **Quarterfinals:** 8 teams → 4 teams
- **Semifinals:** 4 teams → 2 teams
- **Finals:** 2 teams → Champion

Seeding is based on regular-season performance. Tiebreakers, in order:

1. **Week record**
2. **Set win percentage**
3. **Game win percentage**

At the end of the playoffs, one team is crowned **Team Tour champion**.
